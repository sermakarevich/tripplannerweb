Zakopane

stop · PL

Nestled at the foot of the jagged Tatra Mountains, Zakopane is Poland's premier alpine escape, offering dramatic pine-forested hiking trails, historic wooden architecture, and scenic cable car rides.

Stay: €80-150/n town center — cheaper in surrounding villages like Kościelisko €50-90/n

Try: Oscypek (smoked sheep cheese with cranberry jam), Kwaśnica (sour rye soup), Placek po zbójnicku (potato pancake with beef goulash)

  • Kasprowy Wierch Cable Car ~€25-35 · Highly recommended to book online days in advance to bypass massive ticket office queues.
  • Tatra National Park Entry ~€2 · Buy online via the mPay app or official site to skip the cash-only queues at trailheads.
  • Where to eat
  • Cristina Ristorante & Pizzeria — Excellent Italian dishes, fresh seafood, and wood-fired pizzas in a stylish setting, ~€20-35/pp.
  • Drukarnia Smaku Cristina — Upscale restaurant specializing in dry-aged steaks, slow-cooked meats, and modern Polish cuisine, ~€30-50/pp.
  • Karczma Przy Młynie — Traditional highlander tavern serving hearty local meats and trout by a historic mill wheel, ~€15-25/pp.
  • In & around
  • Morskie Oko — 40 min drive to trailhead + 2h walk, a breathtaking emerald lake framed by towering peaks.
  • Chochołów — 25 min drive, a picturesque village of traditional 19th-century wooden log houses.
  • Bachledka Treetop Walk (Slovakia) — 50 min drive, a scenic canopy walk with panoramic views of the Belianske Tatras.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • You must pre-book parking online for Palenica Białczańska (Morskie Oko trailhead); drive-ups without a reservation are rejected.
  • Krupówki Street is heavily crowded and commercialized; explore Kościeliska Street for authentic 19th-century wooden villas.
  • Mountain weather is highly unpredictable; carry layers and rain gear even if the valley looks perfectly sunny.
  • Interesting facts
  • Zakopane developed its own unique architectural style, 'Zakopane Style', created by Stanisław Witkiewicz to resist Swiss chalet influences.
  • The local highlanders, known as Górale, speak a distinct dialect and maintain rich, unique musical and culinary traditions.

Tatranská Lomnica

stop · SK

Nestled under the towering Lomnický štít, Tatranská Lomnica is the ultimate alpine gateway offering dramatic cable car rides, fragrant pine forest hikes, and crisp mountain air.

Stay: €100-180/n central alpine hotels — cheaper pensions in Veľká Lomnica €60-90/n

Try: Tatranský čaj (herbal liqueur), Bryndzové halušky (potato dumplings with sheep cheese), Kapustnica (cabbage soup)

  • Lomnický štít Cable Car ~€50-70 · Extremely popular; book weeks in advance via Gopass as tickets sell out daily.
  • Belianska Cave ~€10 · Tickets purchased on-site; cash only. Prepare for 800+ stairs and cold temperatures inside.
  • Where to eat
  • Olivia Restaurant — Upscale Italian-inspired dining featuring premium steaks, fresh pasta, and excellent wine pairings, ~€25-45/pp.
  • Pizzeria La Romantica — Cozy alpine spot serving wood-fired pizzas, seafood pasta, and grilled meats, ~€15-30/pp.
  • In & around
  • Štrbské Pleso — 30 min drive, stunning glacial lake with pine-fringed walking paths and boat rentals.
  • AquaCity Poprad — 20 min drive, geothermal water park featuring thermal pools and wellness facilities.
  • Ždiar — 20 min drive, a traditional Goral village famous for its painted wooden houses and folk architecture.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • A digital vignette is mandatory for driving on Slovak highways (D1) to reach the Tatras region.
  • Trailhead parking lots in Tatranská Lomnica fill up by 8:00 AM in summer and require daily parking fees.
  • Mountain weather is highly unpredictable; carry waterproof layers even during sunny forecasts.
  • Interesting facts
  • The cable car from Skalnaté Pleso to Lomnický štít was once the most modern in Europe and operates without any support towers on its 1.8 km span.
  • Tatranská Lomnica was established in 1892 as the first state-owned resort and climatic spa in the High Tatras.

Tihany

stop · HU

A dramatic volcanic peninsula jutting into Lake Balaton, famous for its historic hilltop Benedictine Abbey, vibrant lavender fields, and panoramic views of the turquoise waters.

Stay: €90-150/n village center or lakeside — cheaper in nearby Balatonfüred €60-100/n

Try: Balaton fogas (pike-perch), Tihany lavender liqueur, Hungarian goulash, Kürtőskalács (chimney cake)

  • Tihany Benedictine Abbey ~€8 · Buy tickets online in advance during summer to skip the long ticket booth queues.
  • Szántód-Tihany Ferry ~€5/person + €15/car · Essential for crossing to the southern shore; tickets are purchased directly at the port on a first-come, first-served basis.
  • Where to eat
  • Echo Restaurant & Cafe — Perched on a cliff with breathtaking lake views; features grilled pike-perch and lavender-infused desserts, ~€25-40/pp.
  • Mauro Ristorante (Balatonfüred) — Located 15 minutes away; highly popular for authentic Italian wood-fired pizzas, fresh seafood pasta, and steaks, ~€20-45/pp.
  • Apátsági Rege Cukrászda — Historic cafe next to the Abbey offering legendary lavender cream cakes and artisanal coffee with a view, ~€10-15/pp.
  • In & around
  • Balatonfüred — 15 min drive, elegant lakeside promenade, historic villas, and sandy beaches.
  • Badacsony — 45 min drive, volcanic basalt mountains with excellent hiking trails and panoramic wine terraces.
  • Sajkod Beach — 10 min drive, a quieter, pine-fringed beach on the western side of the peninsula perfect for relaxing.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Parking in Tihany village is strictly regulated and paid; use the large designated parking lots at the entrance of the village to avoid fines.
  • Mosquitoes can be highly active near the lake shores and inner lakes during summer evenings; bring strong repellent.
  • The peninsula is highly pedestrianized; wear comfortable walking shoes as the historic center has steep, cobbled streets.
  • Interesting facts
  • The founding charter of the Tihany Abbey (1055) contains the oldest surviving written words in the Hungarian language.
  • Tihany's unique microclimate makes it ideal for lavender cultivation, a tradition imported from France in the 1920s.
  • The peninsula features two inner lakes, Belső-tó and Külső-tó, which are actually water-filled volcanic craters.

Bled

stop · SI

A fairytale alpine lake flanked by dense pine forests and the Julian Alps, featuring a cliffhanging castle and Slovenia's only natural island.

Stay: €130-220/n lakefront — cheaper in surrounding villages like Lesce €80-130

Try: Blejska kremšnita (Bled cream cake), Carniolan sausage, buckwheat štruklji

  • Bled Castle ~€17 · Buy online to skip the ticket booth queue during peak summer hours.
  • Pletna Boat to Bled Island ~€18 · Paid in cash directly to the oarsman; includes round-trip transport.
  • Vogel Cable Car ~€29 · Located in nearby Bohinj, offers panoramic views of the Julian Alps.
  • Where to eat
  • Restavracija Sova Bled — Excellent lakeside seafood, truffle pasta, and premium steaks. ~€35-50/pp
  • Oštarija Peglez'n — Cozy rustic spot serving great Italian-style pasta, fresh Adriatic seafood, and local trout. ~€25-40/pp
  • Restavracija Julijana — Fine dining overlooking the lake, featuring gourmet local meats and refined Mediterranean dishes. ~€60-90/pp
  • In & around
  • Vintgar Gorge — 10 min drive, dramatic wooden boardwalks over the Radovna River rapids.
  • Lake Bohinj & Vogel Cable Car — 30 min drive, a wilder alpine lake surrounded by pine forests and high peaks.
  • Radovljica — 15 min drive, a charming medieval town famous for its chocolate, honeybee museum, and art galleries.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Parking around Lake Bled is strictly regulated and expensive; use designated P+R lots if your hotel doesn't provide parking.
  • A Slovenian vignette is mandatory for driving on all motorways; buy a digital vignette (e-vinjeta) before crossing the border.
  • Bled Island's church has a strict dress code; cover shoulders and knees if you plan to enter and ring the wishing bell.
  • Interesting facts
  • Bled Island is home to the 17th-century Church of the Assumption, where grooms traditionally carry their brides up 99 stone steps for good luck.
  • The famous Bled Cream Cake (Kremšnita) has a protected geographical indication and has been made using the same recipe since 1953.

Piran

stop · SI

A Venetian-Gothic gem jutting into the Adriatic, Piran charms with its car-free medieval alleys, vibrant seafood scene, and dramatic coastal hiking trails along the Strunjan cliffs.

Stay: €110-190/n historic center — cheaper in nearby Portorož €80-130/n

Try: Piran sea bass in salt crust, Istrian truffle pasta, fritto misto, local Malvazija white wine

  • St. George's Parish Church Bell Tower ~€3 · Pay in cash at the entrance; climb the narrow wooden stairs for 360-degree views of three countries.
  • Secovlje Salina Nature Park ~€8 · Visit the active salt pans where salt is still harvested using 700-year-old manual methods.
  • Where to eat
  • Fritolin pri Cantini — Freshly fried calamari, sea bass, and local seafood served open-air in a romantic, rustic square (~€15-25/pp).
  • Gostilna Pri Mari — Excellent family-run tavern serving homemade Istrian pasta with truffles, fresh fish, and premium steaks (~€30-50/pp).
  • Gostilna Pavel — Waterfront dining famous for its seafood platters, lobster pasta, and prime meat dishes right on the promenade (~€25-45/pp).
  • In & around
  • Strunjan Nature Reserve — 10 min drive, featuring hiking trails through pine forests and the highest flysch cliffs in the Adriatic.
  • Portorož Beach — 5 min drive, offering one of Slovenia's few sandy beaches, seaside resorts, and thermal spas.
  • Slavnik Mountain — 45 min drive, a popular hiking peak offering panoramic views stretching from the Adriatic to the Alps.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Piran is completely car-free; you must park in the designated Fornače or Arze parking garages outside town and take the free shuttle bus in.
  • A digital highway vignette (e-vinjeta) is mandatory for driving on Slovenian motorways; purchase it online before crossing the border.
  • Most swimming spots in Piran are rocky or concrete paved; bring water shoes for safety and comfort when entering the sea.
  • Interesting facts
  • Piran was ruled by the Venetian Republic for over 500 years, leaving a legacy of Venetian-Gothic architecture and a bilingual Italian-Slovenian community.
  • The town's main square is named after Giuseppe Tartini, the famous 18th-century violinist and composer who was born in Piran.

Grado

stop · IT

An elegant historic island town connected by a causeway, Grado charms with its Venetian-style old town, shallow sandy beaches, and proximity to both peaceful lagoon wetlands and dramatic alpine peaks.

Stay: €120-190/n historic center — cheaper in Grado Pineta €80-120/n

Try: Boreto alla Graesana (turbot stew with white polenta), Sardele in Saor, local Friulano white wine

  • Aquileia Basilica and Archaeological Museum ~€10 · Combined ticket covers the spectacular 4th-century floor mosaics and the museum.
  • Monte Lussari Cable Car (Tarvisio) ~€14 · Provides rapid access to alpine hiking trails and panoramic mountain views; check seasonal opening dates.
  • Grado Lagoon Boat Tour to Barbana Island ~€15 · Runs regularly in summer; pre-booking is advised on weekends.
  • Where to eat
  • Tavernetta all'Androna — Upscale seafood restaurant in the old town serving creative takes on traditional lagoon fish dishes, ~€60-80/pp
  • Trattoria de Toni — Historic eatery famous for authentic Boreto and fresh local seafood platters, ~€50-70/pp
  • Ristorante Alla Ponente — Excellent spot for both fresh grilled fish and premium steaks with sunset views over the water, ~€45-65/pp
  • In & around
  • Aquileia Roman Ruins — 15 min drive, UNESCO-listed ancient Roman city with massive mosaic floors
  • Riserva Naturale Valle Canal Novo — 25 min drive, hiking trails and birdwatching boardwalks through protected wetlands
  • Tarvisio & Julian Alps — 1h 45m drive, alpine hiking, pine forests, and the scenic Monte Lussari cable car
  • Notes — read before you go
  • The historic center is a pedestrian-only ZTL zone; park in the blue-lined spaces outside and pay via the EasyPark app.
  • Mosquitoes from the lagoon can be highly active during summer evenings; pack strong insect repellent.
  • Many local restaurants close between lunch and dinner (typically 3:00 PM to 7:00 PM), so plan your dining times accordingly.
  • Interesting facts
  • Grado was founded in the 5th century as a safe-haven port for the nearby Roman metropolis of Aquileia.
  • The island's unique microclimate and therapeutic marine thermal baths made it a popular health resort for the Austro-Hungarian aristocracy.
  • The lagoon of Grado contains over 100 small islands, many of which feature traditional thatched-roof fishermen's huts called 'casoni'.

Zell am See

Try: Zeller See-Reinanke (local lake whitefish), Kaspressknödel, Salzburger Nockerl

  • Schmittenhöhe Cable Car ~€35 · Check if your hotel provides the Zell am See-Kaprun Summer Card for free access.
  • Kaprun High Mountain Reservoirs ~€28 · Ticket includes the shuttle bus and the massive Lärchwand inclined elevator.
  • Where to eat
  • Ristorante Pizzeria Giuseppe — Upscale Italian dining featuring fresh seafood pasta, wood-fired pizzas, and excellent Mediterranean fish dishes, ~€30-50/pp.
  • Steinerwirt 1493 — Historic tavern serving premium dry-aged Austrian steaks, local venison, and classic alpine dishes, ~€35-60/pp.

  • In & around
  • Grossglockner High Alpine Road — 30 min drive to Ferleiten toll gate, offering breathtaking panoramic views of Austria's highest peak
  • Sigmund Thun Gorge — 15 min drive, featuring a wooden walkway suspended over a roaring glacial river canyon
  • Kitzsteinhorn Glacier — 20 min drive to Kaprun, offering year-round snow, hiking trails, and a spectacular 3,029m viewing platform
  • Notes — read before you go

  • The Porsche family has a historic estate here, and Ferdinand Porsche himself is buried in the private chapel of the Schüttgut estate in Zell am See.

Hallstatt

stop · AT

Nestled between the dramatic Dachstein Alps and a serene lake, Hallstatt offers breathtaking alpine hiking, a historic salt mine accessed by cable car, and postcard-perfect views.

Stay: €180-300/n lakeside historic center — cheaper in Obertraun €100-160/n

Try: Reinanke (lake trout), Salzburger Nockerl, Austrian Schnitzel

  • Hallstatt Salt Mine (Salzwelten) & Cable Car ~€40 · Book online in advance to secure your funicular and tour time slot.
  • Welterbemuseum Hallstatt ~€10 · Pay at the entrance; showcases 7,000 years of local history.
  • Where to eat
  • Seewirt Zauner — Renowned lakeside hotel restaurant serving fresh, wild-caught lake trout and regional Austrian dishes, ~€30-50/pp.
  • Rudolfsturm — Perched high above the town near the salt mine, offering panoramic views, hearty alpine steaks, and local beers, ~€25-45/pp.
  • Pizzeria Bachti — Cozy local spot serving classic Italian wood-fired pizzas and pasta dishes, ~€15-25/pp.
  • In & around
  • Obertraun Lakeside Park — 10 min drive, features a beach area, pine-fringed shores, and scenic hiking trails.
  • Dachstein Krippenstein — 15 min drive, offers a cable car to the Five Fingers viewing platform and spectacular ice caves.
  • Gosausee — 25 min drive, a pristine alpine lake with hiking paths directly beneath the Dachstein Glacier.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Driving inside the historic center is strictly banned; you must park in designated P1 or P2 parking lots outside the town.
  • The town experiences heavy day-tripper crowds between 10 AM and 4 PM; plan your sightseeing for early morning or late evening.
  • An Austrian motorway vignette is mandatory for your road trip; purchase it online or at gas stations before entering the highway.
  • Interesting facts
  • Hallstatt is home to the world's oldest salt mine, with operations dating back over 7,000 years.
  • A full-scale, historically accurate replica of Hallstatt was constructed in Guangdong, China, in 2012.
  • The local Beinhaus (Charnel House) contains over 600 artistically painted skulls due to limited space in the church graveyard.

Bad Ischl

stop · AT

Step into the imperial summer retreat of the Habsburgs, where historic spa culture meets dramatic Alpine peaks, pine forests, and pristine swimming lakes.

Stay: €120-200/n town center — cheaper in surrounding valleys €80-120

Try: Zaunerstollen, Kaiserschmarrn, Salzkammergut Reinanke (lake trout)

  • Kaiservilla Guided Tour ~€19 · Guided tours of the imperial residence are highly popular; booking online in advance is recommended.
  • Katrin Cable Car (Katrin Seilbahn) ~€26 · Provides access to mountain hiking trails; check operating hours as it closes in bad weather.
  • Where to eat
  • Ristorante Pizzeria da Salvatore — Authentic Italian seafood pasta and wood-fired pizzas in a cozy setting, ~€25-40/pp.
  • KuK Hofbeisl — Historic tavern serving premium local steaks and hearty Austrian classics, ~€30-50/pp.
  • Café Konditorei Zauner — The legendary former supplier to the imperial court, famous for its Zaunerstollen pastry, ~€12-20/pp.
  • In & around
  • Wolfgangsee (St. Gilgen) — 25 min drive, featuring lakeside beaches, pine-fringed shores, and boat cruises.
  • Hallstatt — 30 min drive, the world-famous lakeside village nestled beneath dramatic mountain cliffs.
  • Vorderer Gosausee — 35 min drive, a breathtaking alpine lake with hiking trails and views of the Dachstein Glacier.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • An Austrian highway vignette is required if you use the autobahn to reach the Salzkammergut region.
  • Parking in the historic center is heavily restricted; use the designated P1 or P2 park-and-ride lots.
  • Carry cash, as some smaller cafes, mountain huts, and parking meters do not accept credit cards.
  • Interesting facts
  • Emperor Franz Joseph I spent 82 summers in Bad Ischl and signed the declaration of war that started World War I at his desk in the Kaiservilla.
  • Bad Ischl, along with the Salzkammergut region, was designated as a European Capital of Culture for 2024.

Sopron

stop · HU

A historic Hungarian gem nestled in the pine-covered Lővérek foothills, Sopron charms with its intact medieval old town, rich museum scene, and easy access to the scenic hiking trails of the Sopron Mountains.

Stay: €70-120/n historic center — cheaper near the Lővérek forest €50-80

Try: Kékfrankos (Blaufränkisch) red wine, Soproni Ponczichter bean stew, Strudel

  • Firewatch Tower (Tűztorony) ~€4 · Pay at the entrance; combined tickets with local museums are available.
  • Esterházy Palace (Fertőd) ~€10 · Located 30 mins away; booking ahead is recommended for English-guided tours.
  • Where to eat
  • Erhardt Étterem — Premium local steaks, traditional Hungarian dishes, and excellent Kékfrankos wine pairings ~€25-40/pp.
  • Dömötör Pizzeria és Bisztró — Authentic wood-fired Italian pizzas, fresh pasta, and seafood dishes in a cozy setting ~€15-25/pp.
  • Jégverem Fogadó — Housed in a historic ice cellar, serving massive portions of traditional Hungarian meats and local specialties ~€15-30/pp.
  • In & around
  • Lővérek & Károly Lookout — 10 min drive, hiking trails through dense pine forests leading to a panoramic tower
  • Fertőrákos Quarry — 15 min drive, a spectacular limestone quarry with a cave theater and prehistoric fossil displays
  • Lake Fertő (Neusiedler See) — 20 min drive, offering lakeside relaxation, sailing, and cycling paths along the reed belt
  • Notes — read before you go
  • The historic inner city is a pedestrian-only zone; use the underground parking garage at Petőfi Square just outside the old town walls.
  • If planning to cross into neighboring Austria during your drives, make sure to purchase an Austrian highway vignette online in advance.
  • While major restaurants accept cards, carry some Hungarian Forint (HUF) cash for smaller cafes, local wineries, and parking meters.
  • Interesting facts
  • Sopron is called the 'Most Loyal Town' (Civitas Fidelissima) because its residents voted in a 1921 referendum to remain part of Hungary instead of joining Austria.
  • The local winegrowers were historically nicknamed 'Ponczichter' (bean-growers) because they planted beans between their grapevines to maximize food production.
  • The foundations of Sopron's iconic Firewatch Tower rest on the ancient stone ruins of the Roman city wall of Scarbantia.
