Tatranská Lomnica

Tatranská Lomnica

stop · SK

A premier gateway to the High Tatras, offering dramatic cable car rides to Lomnický štít, lush alpine hiking trails, and a serene mountain resort atmosphere.

Stay: €90-160/n resort center — cheaper guesthouses (pension) €50-90/n

Try: Bryndzové halušky (potato dumplings with sheep cheese), Kapustnica (cabbage soup), Tatratea (herbal liqueur)

  • Lomnický štít Cable Car ~€49-69 · Book weeks in advance via Gopass as tickets to the very peak sell out rapidly; trips are highly weather-dependent.
  • TANAP Museum ~€3 · Tickets purchased at the door; excellent exhibition on Tatra nature and history for rainy days.
  • Where to eat
  • Stará Mama — Traditional Slovak cuisine in a rustic, cozy setting. Try the authentic bryndzové halušky or venison goulash. ~€15-25/pp.
  • Humno Tatry — A unique restaurant and music club featuring a real Cadillac suspended from the ceiling. Famous for grilled ribs and local trout. ~€20-35/pp.
  • Olivia Restaurant — Located in Hotel Kukučka, offering refined modern European dishes and traditional Slovak ingredients with a contemporary twist. ~€30-45/pp.
  • In & around
  • Štrbské Pleso — 30 min drive, iconic glacial lake with scenic walking paths and dramatic mountain backdrops.
  • Belianska Cave — 15 min drive, the only open dripstone cave in the High Tatras featuring stunning stalactites.
  • Starý Smokovec & Hrebienok — 10 min drive, take the funicular railway up for easy hikes to the Cold Water Waterfalls.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • An electronic highway vignette is mandatory if you use the D1 motorway to reach the Tatras; purchase it online at eznamka.sk.
  • Parking in Tatranská Lomnica is strictly regulated; use designated paid central parking lots or ensure your hotel provides a parking permit.
  • Mountain weather changes rapidly; always pack waterproof layers, thermal wear, and sturdy hiking boots even for short summer walks.
  • Interesting facts
  • The cable car to Lomnický štít (2,634m) was one of the most modern in Europe when completed in 1940, suspended without supporting pylons between the stations.
  • Tatranská Lomnica is home to the headquarters of TANAP, the oldest national park in Slovakia, established in 1949.

Bled

Bled

stop · SI

Nestled in the Julian Alps, Lake Bled captivates with its emerald-green waters, a cliffside medieval castle, and a picture-perfect island church. It is a premier gateway for alpine hiking, lakeside swimming, and exploring dramatic gorges.

Stay: €130-220/n lakeside or town center — cheaper in nearby villages like Lesce €80-120

Try: Bled cream cake (Kremšnita), Blejska grmada, local river trout, Carniolan sausage

  • Bled Castle ~€17 · Includes access to the museum and panoramic terraces; booking ahead avoids peak-hour ticket lines.
  • Pletna Boat to Bled Island ~€18 boat + €12 church · Pletna boats are cash-only; alternatively, you can rent a rowboat to row yourself.
  • Where to eat
  • Restavracija Sova Bled — Fresh lake trout, octopus, and premium Slovenian wines right by the water (~€30-45/pp).
  • Ostarija Peglez'n — Rustic, cozy tavern serving excellent seafood platters and grilled squid (~€25-35/pp).
  • Restavracija Julijana — Michelin-recommended fine dining overlooking the lake, featuring refined local ingredients (~€70-90/pp).
  • In & around
  • Vintgar Gorge — 10 min drive, dramatic wooden walkways over the rushing Radovna River.
  • Lake Bohinj — 30 min drive, a wilder, less commercialized alpine lake perfect for hiking and swimming.
  • Pokljuka Plateau — 25 min drive, dense spruce forests and a starting point for high-altitude mountain hikes.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • A Slovenian e-vignette is mandatory for driving on all motorways; purchase it online before crossing the border.
  • Lakeside parking is extremely limited and expensive; use designated P+R lots further out and walk or take the local shuttle.
  • To beat the heavy summer crowds and midday heat on the popular Mala Osojnica viewpoint hike, start before 7:00 AM.
  • Interesting facts
  • The iconic Bled cream cake (Kremšnita) has a protected geographical status and is traditionally crafted to be exactly 7x7 centimeters.
  • Bled Island is Slovenia's only natural island, featuring 99 stone steps leading up to the church where grooms traditionally carry their brides for good luck.

Heiligenblut

Heiligenblut

stop · AT

Nestled at the foot of the Grossglockner, Austria's highest peak, Heiligenblut is a postcard-perfect alpine village offering dramatic mountain vistas, high-altitude hiking, and access to the famous High Alpine Road.

Stay: €110-190/n village center — cheaper guesthouses in surrounding valley €70-110

Try: Kärntner Kasnudeln (Carinthian cheese noodles), Glocknerlamm (local lamb), Glocknerforelle (alpine trout)

  • Grossglockner High Alpine Road Toll ~€43 · Day ticket covers toll; buy online for quick lane entry. Road is closed in winter.
  • Schareck Cable Car ~€32 · Check operating hours seasonally; free access is often included with the Nationalpark Kärnten Card.
  • Where to eat
  • Restaurant Kirchenwirt — Traditional Carinthian specialties like Kasnudeln and venison stew in a rustic alpine setting, ~€25-40/pp.
  • Alpengasthof Glocknerhaus — Hearty mountain hut food with panoramic views of the Pasterze glacier, try the Kaiserschmarrn, ~€20-35/pp.
  • In & around
  • Pasterze Glacier — 25 min drive, hike down to Austria's longest glacier before it retreats further.
  • Lienz — 45 min drive, a charming medieval sun-drenched town in East Tyrol with museums and cafes.
  • Zell am See — 1 hour 15 min drive (via High Alpine Road), beautiful alpine lake with beach areas and boat trips.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • The Grossglockner High Alpine Road is closed in winter (typically late October to early May) due to heavy snow.
  • An Austrian highway vignette is required for motorways, but the Grossglockner High Alpine Road requires an additional separate toll.
  • Weather changes rapidly at 2,000+ meters; pack layers, windproof gear, and sturdy hiking boots even in mid-summer.
  • Interesting facts
  • The village is named 'Holy Blood' after a relic containing the blood of Christ, allegedly brought here by a Danish knight in 914 AD.
  • The iconic St. Vincent Pilgrimage Church, with the Grossglockner peak perfectly framed behind it, is one of the most photographed sights in Austria.

Zell am See

Zell am See

stop · AT

A stunning alpine town where the crystal-clear Lake Zell meets the dramatic peaks of the Schmittenhöhe, offering a perfect mix of lakeside beaches and high-altitude hiking.

Stay: €150-250/n lakeside or center — cheaper in Schüttdorf €100-150/n

Try: Zeller Reinanke (local lake whitefish), Kasnocken (alpine cheese noodles), Kaiserschmarrn

  • Schmittenhöhe Cable Car ~€35 · Often included for free if your accommodation provides the Zell am See-Kaprun Summer Card.
  • Kaprun High Alpine Reservoirs ~€28 · Ticket includes the transfer buses and the open-air Lärchwand inclined elevator.
  • Where to eat
  • Restaurant Salzburgerhof — Award-winning fine dining featuring freshly caught local lake trout and refined Austrian classics, ~€60-90/pp.
  • Steinerwirt — A historic tavern serving excellent Wiener Schnitzel, local venison, and traditional Salzburger Nockerl, ~€25-40/pp.
  • Kupferkessel — A lively, casual spot popular for its hearty steaks, pizzas, and local draft beers, ~€20-35/pp.
  • In & around
  • Grossglockner High Alpine Road — 30 min drive to the toll gate, offering breathtaking views of Austria's highest peak.
  • Krimml Waterfalls — 50 min drive to Europe's highest waterfalls with a scenic mist-sprayed hiking trail.
  • Sigmund Thun Gorge — 15 min drive to a dramatic wooden walkway suspended over a rushing glacial river.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • An Austrian highway vignette is mandatory for the drive; buy a digital or physical sticker before entering the motorway.
  • Parking in the historic center is heavily restricted; use the designated Contipark underground garages to avoid steep fines.
  • Book accommodation that explicitly includes the 'Zell am See-Kaprun Summer Card' to save significantly on cable cars and lidos.
  • Interesting facts
  • Lake Zell contains exceptionally pure water that is officially certified to drinking water standards.
  • The town's iconic Grand Hotel, built in 1894 on its own private peninsula, has hosted Empress Elisabeth (Sisi) of Austria.

St. Gilgen

St. Gilgen

stop · AT

A picturesque lakeside village on Wolfgangsee, St. Gilgen combines alpine hiking trails, pristine swimming beaches, and deep cultural ties to Mozart's family.

Stay: €130-220/n lakeside or center — cheaper in surrounding hills €90-130

Try: Wolfgangsee Saibling (lake trout), Kaiserschmarrn, Salzburger Nockerl

  • Zwölferhorn Cable Car ~€33 · Buy online to skip the ticket window queue on sunny weekends.
  • Wolfgangsee Schifffahrt (Ferry) ~€24 · Combined tickets with the SchafbergBahn cog railway are available.
  • Mozarthaus St. Gilgen ~€7 · Small museum dedicated to Mozart's mother and sister; check seasonal opening hours.
  • Where to eat
  • Gasthof zur Post — Traditional Austrian tavern serving fresh Wolfgangsee fish and local venison (~€25-40/pp).
  • Fischerstube — Freshly caught lake trout (Saibling) and seafood platters right by the water (~€30-50/pp).
  • Café Dallmann — Mozart-inspired pastries, handmade Mozartkugeln, and traditional coffee (~€10-15/pp).
  • In & around
  • St. Wolfgang & SchafbergBahn — 20 min drive (or ferry), historic cog railway up the mountain.
  • Hallstatt — 40 min drive, iconic lakeside UNESCO village and ancient salt mine.
  • Bad Ischl — 20 min drive, imperial spa town with Kaiservilla and famous Café Zauner.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • An Austrian highway vignette is mandatory if you use the A1 autobahn to get here.
  • Parking near the lakefront (Strandbad) fills up very early on hot summer days; use the designated P1 or P2 park-and-ride lots.
  • Many smaller shops and traditional mountain huts (Almen) only accept cash, so keep Euros on hand.
  • Interesting facts
  • Although Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart never visited St. Gilgen, his mother Anna Maria was born here, and his sister Nannerl lived here for many years.
  • The village was used as a filming location for the opening scenes of the classic movie 'The Sound of Music'.

Hallstatt

Hallstatt

stop · AT

A fairytale alpine village wedged between dramatic Dachstein mountains and a pristine lake, offering world-class salt mine history and stunning mountain hikes.

Stay: €150-250/n lakeside old town — cheaper in Obertraun €90-140/n

Try: Reinanke (lake trout), Salzburger Nockerl, Hallstätter Krapfen

  • Hallstatt Salt Mine (Salzwelten) & Skywalk ~€40 · Book online in advance to skip long funicular lines, especially in summer.
  • Dachstein Krippenstein Cable Car & Ice Cave ~€50-60 · Check weather webcams before booking; mountain weather changes rapidly.
  • Where to eat
  • Restaurant im Seehotel Grüner Baum — Freshly caught Lake Hallstatt whitefish (Reinanke) served on a lakeside terrace. ~€35-50/pp.
  • Gasthof Zauner — Historic inn famous for wild game dishes and grilled local trout. ~€30-45/pp.
  • Rudolfsturm — Traditional Austrian dumplings and strudel served with panoramic views next to the salt mine. ~€20-30/pp.
  • In & around
  • Obertraun Beach & Koppenbrüller Cave — 10 min drive, great swimming spot and a roaring active water cave.
  • Gosau Lakes (Vorderer Gosausee) — 20 min drive, mirror-like alpine lake with Dachstein glacier views and hiking paths.
  • Bad Ischl — 25 min drive, historic spa town featuring the Kaiservilla, Emperor Franz Joseph's summer residence.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Day-tripper crowds peak between 10:00 and 16:00; explore early in the morning or late in the evening for a peaceful experience.
  • Cars are strictly banned in the historic center; you must park in designated external lots (P1 or P2) and use the shuttle if staying overnight.
  • Austria requires a digital or physical highway vignette for your road trip; purchase it before crossing the border or at gas stations.
  • Interesting facts
  • Hallstatt is home to the world's oldest salt mine, dating back over 7,000 years to the Bronze Age.
  • The village is so beloved in Asia that a full-scale replica of Hallstatt was built in Guangdong, China.
  • Due to limited space in the local graveyard, bones in the Beinhaus (Charnel House) were historically exhumed, bleached, and hand-painted with floral motifs.

Bad Ischl

Bad Ischl

stop · AT

The elegant heart of the Salzkammergut lake district, Bad Ischl combines imperial Habsburg grandeur with pristine alpine nature, thermal waters, and dramatic mountain peaks.

Stay: €120-200/n town center — cheaper guesthouses in the surrounding valleys €80-120

Try: Zaunerstollen, Kaiserschmarrn, Salzkammergut Reinanke (freshly caught lake trout)

  • Kaiservilla (Imperial Villa) ~€22 · Guided tours of the imperial apartments are highly recommended and can sell out during peak summer hours.
  • Katrin Cable Car (Katrin Seilbahn) ~€26 · Provides access to the 7-Lakes-View hiking trail; check operating hours as it closes in bad weather.
  • Where to eat
  • Café Konditorei Zauner — The legendary former supplier to the imperial court; try the iconic Zaunerstollen nougat cake, ~€10-15/pp.
  • Restaurant Weinhaus Attwenger — Historic riverside restaurant serving excellent local lake fish and traditional Austrian dishes, ~€25-45/pp.
  • k.u.k. Hofwirt — Classic tavern atmosphere offering hearty Austrian staples like Tafelspitz and Wiener Schnitzel, ~€20-35/pp.
  • In & around
  • Hallstatt — 25 min drive, the world-famous lakeside village with its ancient salt mine and dramatic mountain backdrop.
  • Wolfgangsee (St. Gilgen) — 20 min drive, offering beautiful lakeside beaches, swimming spots, and boat cruises.
  • Gosauseen Lakes — 35 min drive, stunning alpine lakes reflecting the jagged peaks of the Dachstein Glacier.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • An Austrian digital or sticker vignette is mandatory if you use the autobahn (A1) to reach the Salzkammergut region.
  • Parking in the historic center is strictly regulated; use the designated long-term parking lots (P1 or P2) to avoid heavy fines.
  • Many traditional mountain huts and local cafes in the area do not accept credit cards, so always carry cash.
  • Interesting facts
  • Emperor Franz Joseph I and Empress Sisi spent 60 summers here, and the town is where they officially got engaged in 1853.
  • Bad Ischl was designated as a European Capital of Culture in 2024, celebrating its rich alpine heritage and contemporary art.
  • It is Austria's oldest saltwater spa town, utilizing the region's natural saline springs for therapeutic treatments since 1823.

Český Krumlov

Český Krumlov

stop · CZ

A fairytale medieval town looped by the Vltava River, featuring a massive castle complex, winding cobblestone lanes, and easy access to the dense forests and lakes of South Bohemia.

Stay: €80-150/n historic center — cheaper guesthouses (penzion) €50-80 outside the river bend

Try: South Bohemian trout (pstruh), garlic soup (česnečka), local Eggenberg beer, gingerbread

  • Český Krumlov Castle Tours ~€12 · Book the Castle Theater and Tower tours well in advance as spots are strictly limited.
  • Egon Schiele Art Centrum ~€8 · Dedicated to the Austrian modernist painter; tickets can usually be bought at the door.
  • Where to eat
  • Krčma v Šatlavské ulici — Medieval-style tavern serving open-fire grilled meats and traditional Bohemian platters, ~€20-30/pp.
  • Papa's Living Restaurant — Riverside dining featuring fresh local river trout, pasta, and great local wines, ~€25-35/pp.
  • Laibon — Creative vegetarian and local dishes served on a peaceful terrace right on the Vltava River, ~€15-25/pp.
  • In & around
  • Lipno Reservoir — 30 min drive, sandy lakeside beaches, sailing, and a treetop walkway.
  • Kleť Mountain — 20 min drive, hike or take the chairlift up for panoramic views and an observatory.
  • Hluboká Castle — 40 min drive, a stunning Windsor-style white neo-Gothic chateau.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • The historic center is a pedestrian-only zone; you must park in designated outer lots (P1-P4) and walk in.
  • Day-trippers flood the narrow streets from 10 AM to 4 PM; explore early in the morning or late in the evening for a peaceful experience.
  • Many smaller shops and traditional taverns still prefer cash (Czech Koruna - CZK), though cards are widely accepted in larger venues.
  • Interesting facts
  • The castle moat has been home to brown bears since the 16th century, a tradition maintained to this day.
  • The town's Baroque Castle Theater is one of the world's best-preserved, complete with its original 18th-century stage machinery and scenery.

Mikulov

Mikulov

stop · CZ

Dominated by a majestic clifftop castle, Mikulov is a picturesque South Moravian wine town nestled against the white limestone cliffs of the Pálava hills.

Stay: €70-120/n historic center — cheaper guesthouses in suburbs €50-80

Try: Pálava white wine, Moravian smoked meats, pickled Hermelín cheese

  • Mikulov Castle ~€10 · Guided tours of the historic wine cellars and the giant barrel are highly recommended on weekends.
  • Dietrichstein Tomb ~€6 · Located right on the main square; check seasonal opening hours as it closes in winter.
  • Where to eat
  • Sojka & spol. — Modern bistro serving seasonal Moravian dishes and an exceptional selection of local wines — ~€20-30/pp.
  • Bistro Drogerka — Popular spot on the main square known for great breakfasts, light lunches, and specialty coffee — ~€10-15/pp.
  • Kukanz — Cozy restaurant offering traditional Czech classics and local wines in a stylishly rustic interior — ~€15-25/pp.
  • In & around
  • Pálava Hills (Děvín) — 10 min drive, scenic hiking trails through limestone cliffs leading to the Děvičky castle ruins.
  • Lednice-Valtice Complex — 15 min drive, a breathtaking UNESCO site featuring romantic châteaux, English gardens, and boating canals.
  • Nové Mlýny Reservoirs — 15 min drive, vast lakes offering windsurfing, beaches, and lakeside cycling paths.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Parking in the historic center is strictly regulated and requires paying via automated meters or a mobile app.
  • A Czech highway vignette is mandatory if you use the nearby D2 highway to access the region.
  • Many smaller local wine cellars (sklepy) and family vineyards operate on a cash-only basis, so carry Czech Koruna (CZK).
  • Interesting facts
  • Mikulov Castle houses one of the largest historic wine barrels in Europe, built in 1643 with a capacity of over 101,000 liters.
  • The town was historically a major center of Jewish culture in Moravia, serving as the seat of the regional land rabbi for over three centuries.

Terchová

Terchová

stop · SK

Gateway to the dramatic Malá Fatra National Park, Terchová is a picturesque mountain village famed for its rugged gorges, traditional folklore, and as the birthplace of Slovakia's legendary outlaw Juraj Jánošík.

Stay: €60-100/n in local guesthouses (pension) — premium wellness hotels €120-180/n

Try: Bryndzové halušky (potato dumplings with sheep cheese), Terchovský korbáčik (braided string cheese), Kapustnica (sauerkraut soup)

  • Vrátna Chleb Cable Car ~€18 · Check weather conditions and seasonal operating hours online before heading up to the ridge.
  • Terchová Museum (Expozícia Jánošík a Terchová) ~€4 · A small museum dedicated to local history and the legendary outlaw Juraj Jánošík; tickets are purchased on-site.
  • Where to eat
  • Terchovská Koliba Diery — Traditional wooden cabin serving authentic bryndzové halušky and garlic soup in a bread bowl; ~€15-20/pp.
  • Haluškáreň Terchová — Cozy local eatery specializing in various types of traditional Slovak dumplings; ~€10-15/pp.
  • In & around
  • Orava Castle — 45 min drive, a stunning medieval castle perched high on a rock above the Orava River.
  • Šútovo Waterfall — 30 min drive, a scenic hike through the forest to the highest waterfall in the Malá Fatra range.
  • Strečno Castle — 25 min drive, ruined medieval fortress offering panoramic views over the Váh river gorge.
  • Notes — read before you go
  • Parking at the main trailheads like Biely Potok fills up very early on summer weekends; bring cash for parking fees (~€5-10).
  • The ladders and metal chains in the Jánošíkove Diery gorges can be extremely slippery after rain; sturdy hiking boots are essential.
  • Slovakia requires an electronic highway vignette (e-známka) for driving on motorways; purchase it online before entering the country.
  • Interesting facts
  • Terchová is the birthplace of Juraj Jánošík, the 'Slovak Robin Hood', who is celebrated with a massive 7.5-meter steel statue overlooking the village.
  • The traditional music of Terchová, characterized by multi-part singing and string instrument play, was inscribed on the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2013.
